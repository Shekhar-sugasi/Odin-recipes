Lumpia

Description

Lumpia is a traditional Filipino spring roll that is often filled with a mixture of ground meat and vegetables, wrapped in a thin pastry, and deep-fried to a crispy perfection.
It is commonly served as an appetizer or snack and is enjoyed with a dipping sauce.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground pork or beef
  • 1 medium carrot, finely chopped
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup shredded cabbage
  • 1/4 cup green onions, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 30 lumpia wrappers
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, combine the ground meat, chopped carrot, onion, garlic, shredded cabbage, and green onions, and mix until well combined.
  2. Place a lumpia wrapper on a flat surface and spoon about 1-2 tablespoons of the filling onto one corner of the wrapper.
  3. Fold the corner of the wrapper over the filling, roll tightly halfway, fold in the sides. Moisten the end with a bit of water to seal completely.
  4. Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan or wok over medium heat.
  5. Drain the lumpia on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  6. Serve hot with your favorite dipping sauce, such as sweet chili sauce or vinegar with garlic.
